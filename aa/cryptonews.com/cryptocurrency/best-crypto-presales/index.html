What Is a Crypto Presale?


Crypto presales, also known as Inicial Coin Offerings (ICOs), are usually the first time investors can buy tokens. This allows them to secure coins before they list on crypto exchanges. Depending on the project, the presale can be public or private.

During a presale, crypto tokens are typically sold at a highly discounted price. For example, Ethereumâ€™s presale in 2014 sold pre-mined ETH for under $0.30. By 2021, it hit an all-time high of nearly $4,900.

The objective of a crypto presale is to raise funds for development and generate interest in the project. Some projects reward early investors with premium memberships, additional tokens, and access to exclusive features.

Crypto Presale Roadmap

A crypto presale is often structured in stages to track sales and fundraising progress, with prices typically rising as stages advance.

Following the presale or ICO, projects can launch their tokens via an IDO or IEO. Each method aims to broaden market exposure and potentially increase token prices.

  • Initial DEX Offering (IDO): Tokens are launched on a decentralized exchange by creating a liquidity pool. This method facilitates immediate trading and is known for its transparency and reduced risk of manipulation.
  • Initial Exchange Offering (IEO): Tokens are launched on a centralized exchange. The platform vets the projects and handles the sale, providing a layer of trust and security for investors.

Successful projects may seek further listing on more centralized exchanges to expand their reach and enhance liquidity, potentially accelerating price appreciation compared to tokens only available on decentralized platforms.

Key Takeaways on Crypto Presales

  • A crypto presale offers early investors tokens at a discounted rate, similar to crowdfunding for new crypto projects.
  • Buying tokens during a presale can lead to significant profits due to the low initial investment.
  • Unclear regulations create uncertainty for both crypto projects and investors.
  • Risks for investors include potential scams and projects failing to compete effectively.

Famous Cryptos That Had Presales


Several prominent blockchains originated from presales. Here are some of the most well-known examples:

  • Ethereum (ETH): Launched in 2014, Ethereum’s presale offered Ether at an exchange rate of 2,000 ETH for 1 Bitcoin, meaning each ETH token costed roughly $0.30. It hit an all-time hight (ATH) of $4,878 in November 2021.
  • TRON (TRX): TRON’s ICO in 2018 priced its token at $0.0019. It later peaked at $0.3004, reflecting substantial growth from its presale price.
  • BNB (Binance Coin): Started in 2017 with an exchange rate of 2,700 BNB for 1 ETH, about $0.15 per BNB at the time. By 2021, BNB had soared to nearly $700, marking it one of the most successful early crypto investments.
  • Solana (SOL): Known for its speed and low-cost transactions, Solana had multiple funding rounds beginning in 2018 with a presale price of $0.20 for SOL. It reached an ATH of $260.06 by late 2021.
