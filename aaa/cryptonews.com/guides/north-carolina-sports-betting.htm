Who Can Partake in North Carolina Sports Betting?


Who’s allowed to bet on sports in North Carolina? We outline the requirements below.

Legal Age

In North Carolina, you must be at least 21 to engage in in-person and online sports betting.

Mobile Betting

North Carolina recently legalized mobile sports betting, and you’ll be able to place bets online sometime between January and June 2024.

Remote Registration

Unlike brick-and-mortar sportsbooks where you don’t need to register, you’ll need to make an account to participate in online sports betting. You will likely need to provide proof of residency to use North Carolina-based sportsbooks.

Within State Borders

You must reside within North Carolina to place bets at state-approved sportsbooks.

Retail Sportsbooks

Taxable Winnings

North Carolina considers sports betting winnings as income, so these winnings are taxed at the individual income tax rate of 4.75%. You’ll have to pay the same tax rate for online sports betting earnings.

How To Start Betting in North Carolina


If you’ve never placed a sports bet online before, it can be tough to know where to start. We guide you through the process below.

Select a Sportsbook

Before you make your bets, you’ll have to choose a sportsbook. This is an important step, as some sportsbooks have better odds and more features than others.

Consider factors such as:

  • What are the odds? Some sportsbooks strongly favor the house, which means you have poorer chances of winning.
  • What are the sportsbook’s reviews like? If they’re mostly negative, move on.
  • Is the sportsbook well-organized and easy to navigate?
  • Does it offer a bonus for new customers?
  • Does it charge fees for deposits and withdrawals?
  • What payment methods does it accept?
  • How fast are the payouts?

Register Online

Once you’ve picked a sportsbook, you’ll have to make an account (except some Telegram-based sportsbooks, which require no sign-up).

Some sportsbooks just ask for your email address, while others require proof of identity and residence. It may take the sportsbook a few days to review your documents.

Claim a Bonus

Virtually all online sportsbooks offer some kind of welcome bonus. Many apply the bonus automatically if you meet the requirements. For others, you might need to enter a promo code.

Deposit Funds

Next, make your first deposit. This is easy if the sportsbook accepts credit/debit, e-wallets, or bank transfers.

Some sportsbooks only accept crypto, such as Bitcoin, Dogecoin, and Ethereum. If you must use crypto to bet, you can usually buy cryptocurrency directly through the sportsbook.

Your chosen sportsbook might have a minimum deposit. Most crypto casinos have no or low minimum deposits. If you deposit via credit card or bank transfer, you might have to spend $100 or more.

Choose Your Bet Types

You can choose many different kinds of sports bets, and most reputable sportsbooks offer all the most popular kinds. They include:

  • Futures: Put money on bets that will be decided in the future, such as next year’s Super Bowl.
  • Totals: Also called over/under bets. With this bet, you put money on the final score of a game.
  • Spreads: Spreads allow you to bet on a certain number of touchdowns, home runs, goals, points, etc. If you bet for the underdog, it’s called “taking” the spread. If you bet for the favorite, that’s called “giving” the spread.
  • Props: With props, you bet on an individual player’s performance.
  • Parlays: Parlays let you combine two or more straight wagers into a single bet.

Start Betting

Placing a bet is easy once you know which bet type you want. The process varies by sportsbook a bit, but works like this:

  1. Choose a game and bet type.
  2. Click or tap the bet cell. The cell will be added to your bet slip.
  3. Enter the amount you want to bet.
  4. Choose “Submit” or “Place Bet.”

Withdraw Funds

Once you’ve won a bet or two, you’re ready to make a withdrawal. Not so fast, though: Some sportsbooks impose withdrawal minimums, which you’ll have to meet first.

If you do meet the minimum, just head to the sportsbook’s banking page and choose your withdrawal amount. You’ll also have to pick a withdrawal method. Some sportsbooks only let you withdraw to a crypto wallet, while others accept withdrawals to bank accounts and credit cards.

Crypto withdrawals are often free. If you pick another method, you might have to pay a fee.

Reading the Odds

Sportsbook odds can be complicated, but we’ll try to break it down as simply as possible.

Most good online sportsbooks use American odds, with a plus sign denoting how much you could win with a $100 bet and a minus sign that depicts how much you’d have to bet to win $100.

Let’s say the Dallas Cowboys have +700 odds to win the Super Bowl. If you bet $20 on the Cowboys and they win, you could earn $140 (700x$20).

Now let’s assume the Los Angeles Dodgers have -150 odds to win the World Series. In this case, you’d have to bet $20 to win $30 ($20×1.5).

Responsible Gaming in North Carolina


The North Carolina State Lottery Commission recognizes how devastating problem gambling can be for residents. As such, it has outlined several rules for sports betting operators:

  • They must verify bettors’ identities to make sure they’re over 21
  • They must not engage in marketing to people below the legal gambling age
  • They must have a voluntary exclusion program to allow bettors to limit their gambling
  • They must provide responsible gambling training to employees

North Carolina Sports Betting History


North Carolina was one of the first states to legalize brick-and-mortar sports wagering on tribal lands with the signing of Senate Bill 154 in 2019. The state restricted all sports betting to wagering in-person at tribal casinos, and the first bets weren’t placed until 2021.

Lawmakers tried to make online sports betting legal in 2022, but failed. They finally succeeded in 2023 when Governor Roy Cooper signed House Bill 347 into law.

This new law gives the North Carolina State Lottery Commission a five-month window (from January to June 2024) to establish online sports betting.
