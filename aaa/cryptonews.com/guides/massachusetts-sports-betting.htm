Massachusetts Sports Betting Updates


The most prominent Massachusetts sports betting update occurred on March 10, 2023, when online sports betting became legal statewide. Quite a few developments led us to this point, as summarized below:

  • May 2018: The U.S. Supreme Court overturned the Professional and Amateur Sports Protection Act (PASPA), allowing individual states to decide whether or not they would legalize sports betting.
  • August 10, 2022: Massachusetts Governor Charlie Baker signed Bill H.5164, the Massachusetts Sports Wagering Act, into law, officially legalizing sports betting in Massachusetts.
  • January 31, 2023: Sports betting in Massachusetts finally began with the state’s first three casinos (Encore Boston Harbor, MGM Springfield, and Plainridge Park Casino) opening their doors to players.
  • March 10, 2023: Massachusetts online sports betting officially launched at 10 a.m. as the first six digital sportsbooks that acquired licenses commenced virtual betting.

Is Sports Betting Legal in Massachusetts?

Yes, sports betting became legal in Massachusetts after former Governor Charlie Baker signed House Bill 5164 into law on August 10, 2022, allowing retail wagering to launch by January 2023 and online wagering to officially begin in March 2023. While sports betting may be legal in Massachusetts, players must follow a few parameters to stay within the state’s legislative boundaries. For example, you must be over 21, within the state’s borders, and on a licensed sportsbook if you want to place your bets legally.

Who Can Partake in Massachusetts Sports Betting?


If you’re interested in betting on your favorite team in Massachusetts, you must still meet a few eligibility requirements to remain within legal boundaries. Massachusetts requires the following:

Legal Age

You must be 21 or older to legally bet on sports in Massachusetts. While some U.S. states have the betting age set at 18, Massachusetts requires players to be at least 21 before betting on their favorite teams.

Mobile Betting

If you want to bet on sports legally, you must use a registered Massachusetts sports betting site. Right now, only eight legal sportsbooks are available, but the state will grant licenses to more in the future. Some operators will be able to partner with land-based casinos so you can play online or at your favorite casino.

Remote Registration

You must register an account with a legal Massachusetts sportsbook before you can begin playing for identity verification purposes. The state allows you to register your account and place bets from anywhere within state borders.

Within State Borders

You must be within Massachusetts state borders to place bets online using a sportsbook. Whether you’re visiting Massachusetts on vacation or live here permanently, you can enjoy the newly granted sports betting freedom, assuming you place your wagers on Massachusetts land.

Retail Sportsbooks

Massachusetts currently has three legal retail sportsbooks, including Encore Boston Harbor, Plainridge Park, and MGM Springfield. If you’d like to place your bets in person, you must visit one of these facilities. A couple of pari-mutuel facilities, including Raynham Park and Suffolk Downs, will also open their doors to legal sports betting soon.

Taxable Winnings

You must pay taxes on any of your sports betting earnings, just as you would with gambling income. Massachusetts imposes a 5% state tax on top of the 24% federal tax rate. The federal W-2G form will be triggered if you cash out more than $600, though keep in mind that your losses will offset your earnings.

Massachusetts sports betting has its advantages and disadvantages. Whether you’re a resident in the state celebrating the recent legislation or someone hoping to travel to Massachusetts to take advantage of the new laws, you should understand the pros and cons before placing your wagers.

Massachusetts Sports Betting History


We know that sports betting is legal in Massachusetts, but how did the state get to this point?

Throughout 2019, lawmakers attempted to submit multiple sports betting bills following the Supreme Court overturning that allowed states to make their own decisions on the matter, though none of these bills passed. In 2020, a bill that incorporated sports betting into the state’s economic development made it through the House but still couldn’t pass through the Senate. By 2021, roughly 23 sports betting bills were filed between the House and Senate, with H.3977 gaining a 156-3-1 vote in the lower chamber and support from Gov. Charlie Baker, yet it still never passed.

After years of disagreements, lawmakers finally settled on H.5164, which passed through the Senate by a 36-4 vote on August 1, 2022, before being signed into law by Gov. Baker on August 10, 2022, officially legalizing sports betting in Massachusetts.
