If you’re looking for information on Texas sports betting, you may have a lot of questions. Is online sports betting in Texas legal? Where can you sports bet in Texas? What sports betting apps are legal in Texas?

Whether you are an experienced sports bettor or a novice just getting started, we answer all these questions and more so you know about the state of sports betting in Texas in 2024. We will discuss popular sports to bet on, legal sports betting options for Texans, and Texas sports betting updates.

Is Sports Betting Legal in Texas?


Sports betting is not currently legal in Texas. Despite calls from sports fans, professional teams, and even some lawmakers for the legalization of sports betting, Texas has some of the strictest anti-gambling laws in the country. As recently as December 7, 2023, Texas Lieutenant Governor Dan Patrick reiterated that the Senate does not support casino gambling in the state.

Latest Texas Betting News


The most recent news in the world of betting in the Lone Star State is that Texans can now legally play online casino games in Oklahoma. This news came in December 2023, when the Chickasaw Nation launched a mobile app that only works at the Winstar World Casino and Resort in Thackerville, OK, near the Oklahoma-Texas line.

The new app allows people to play three popular electronic games on their mobile devices. However, they must play on trust land at Winstar, where the casino verifies their location with geofencing technology. While the Winstar app doesn’t currently allow sports betting, future legislative sessions in Oklahoma will likely address the issue.

Earlier in 2023, several proposals to legalize sports betting in Texas made it through the House of Representatives but did not make it to the state Senate for a vote. The earliest the Senate could address the matter is during the next legislative session in 2025.

Current Legal Status of Sports Betting in Texas


People wishing to engage in sports betting in Texas do not have any legal options available at this time, except on-site betting at racetracks. Unlike many other states, Texas has not taken advantage of recent changes in US federal law to legalize sports betting in the state.

The Texas legislature is considering several bills related to online sports betting in Texas, but they have not yet generated the political support required to pass. In the legislative session of 2023, HB 1942, a bill setting out a framework for legal sports betting, made it through the Texas House. However, Lt. Gov. Patrick said the Senate would not consider the bill, ending hopes for Texas sports betting to become legal in 2023.

Because the next legislative session will not occur until 2025, sports betting will remain illegal in the state until at least then.

Texas Sports Betting Timeline


Although sports betting in Texas remains illegal, numerous fans, teams, and legislators have expressed their support for legalizing sports betting in the state. The following are some of the sports betting and other gambling-related events that made the news in 2023.

  • May 10, 2023: The Texas House approved HJR 102, a proposal to amend the state constitution to allow online sports betting, to move to the Senate. However, Lt. Gov. Patrick said he would not refer it to a vote in the Senate.
  • May 13, 2023: Lt. Gov. Patrick said he will not call HB 1942 to the Senate floor, effectively ending the bill’s chances of passing in 2023.
  • May 29, 2023: The 2023 Texas legislative session ended without any change in the legality of sports betting.
  • September and October 2023: Texas law enforcement carried out multiple raids of illegal gambling rooms in small towns and large cities in the state. Officials made numerous arrests and seized tens of thousands of dollars worth of cash, gambling machines, and paraphernalia.
  • November 7, 2023: Dallas Mavericks owner Mark Cuban again expressed his support for casino gaming at resorts in Texas. He said he hoped to partner with the Las Vegas Sands to build a casino resort complex in Dallas, including a new Mavericks arena. For that to happen, however, the casino laws in Texas would have to change.
  • November 29, 2023: Mark Cuban agreed to sell a majority stake in the team to the family who owns the Las Vegas Sands casino resort.
  • December 7, 2023: Texas Lt. Gov. Patrick reiterated that most lawmakers in the state Senate still do not support legal casino gambling.

The Impact of Recent Legislation on Texas Sports Betting


For the most part, recent legislation that has attempted to change the sports betting landscape in Texas has failed. However, that has not stopped proponents from bringing new proposals to the table. Some of them have even made progress through the Texas legislature.

In 2023, the state legislature considered the following sports betting house bills (HB) and house joint resolutions (HJR):

  • HB 1942: Introduced in February, HB 1942 would have provided sports betting licenses to 16 teams and facilities. With the backing of major sports teams, the bill passed the Texas House easily with a vote of 82-51. In doing so, it marked the first time a sports betting proposal passed either chamber in the Texas legislature. However, Lt. Gov. Patrick later stopped the resolution before the Senate could vote on it, saying it didn’t have the needed support.
  • HJR 102: This resolution proposed a constitutional amendment authorizing the legislature to legalize betting on certain sporting events in Texas. It passed the House, clearing the necessary two-thirds approval with a vote of 101-42 on the fifth round of voting. However, Lt. Gov. Patrick also stopped this bill from advancing to a vote in the Senate.
  • HJR 155: Introduced in March, HJR 155 proposed to let voters decide whether to legalize casinos in Texas. In May, the House voted to postpone the resolution until 2027.

In 2021, the Texas legislature also introduced several other sports betting bills and resolutions, including the following:

  • HB 2070: Introduced in February, this bill would have paved the way for legalizing sports betting in Texas. If approved, the issue would have appeared on ballots before Texas voters in November. However, the House left it pending in committee in April.
  • HJR 97: Also introduced in February, this proposed legislation would have changed the Texas Constitution to legalize casino gambling and sports betting. However, the House left it pending in committee in April.
  • HB 1121: Filed in January, HB 1121 called for a 6.25% tax on online sports gambling in Texas, a legal betting age of 18, and a ban on betting on local college teams. However, it failed to receive a vote.

Popular Sports To Bet on in Texas


Texas has the second-largest population of any US state, with nearly 30 million people. It also has at least one team in each of the “Big Four” professional sports leagues (NFL, NBA, MLB, and NHL).

In fact, Texas has 12 professional teams, including three NBA teams, two NFL teams, two MLB teams, two MLS (Major League Soccer) teams, one NHL team, one WNBA team, and one NWSL (National Women’s Soccer League) team. If sports betting becomes legal in Texas, it would no doubt mean big business for sportsbook operators in the state.

Based on the number of Texas teams and the general popularity of the sports nationwide, the following would likely make for popular Texas sports betting leagues:

National Football League (NFL)

The NFL is by far the preferred sport to bet on for gamblers in the United States. In a recent poll of mobile bettors aged 18 and over, 81% had wagered on the NFL in the previous month. That was over 25% more than the next most popular league, the NBA.

With the general popularity of football in the Lone Star State, that preference is likely to hold for Texas sports bettors as well — especially since the state has two NFL teams. The Dallas Cowboys are popular not only in Texas but also around the country. In addition, the state is home to the Houston Texans, who replaced the Oilers when they moved to Tennessee.

National Basketball Association (NBA)

With three professional teams, basketball is also a big favorite in Texas. The state hosts the San Antonio Spurs, the Houston Rockets, and the Dallas Mavericks, now partially owned by Mark Cuban. As a vocal proponent of sports betting, Cuban recently sold his majority stake in the team to the owners of the Las Vegas Sands casino and resort. He is hoping to build a casino complex and basketball venue for the Mavericks in Dallas.

Basketball makes for popular sports betting outside of Texas as well, as 54% of online gamblers in the US had placed bets on the NBA.

Major League Baseball (MLB)

With two professional baseball teams and 162 games each in a regular season, the MLB is also likely to make for popular sports betting in Texas. The excitement in the state was palpable as the Texas Rangers (based in Arlington) won their first World Series in franchise history in 2023. On the other end of the state, the Houston Astros have won two World Series: one in 2017 and one in 2022.

National Hockey League (NHL)

While the hot Texas weather doesn’t necessarily conjure up images of ice hockey, the NHL also has a home in the state. The Dallas Stars compete in the Central Division in the Western Conference of the NHL. The team has won eight division titles, last appearing in the Stanley Cup Finals in 2020.

Rounding out the Big Four professional leagues, the NHL will likely earn a reputation among Texas sports bettors should online gambling become legal in the state. The NHL came in sixth place in the poll of mobile bettors, above boxing and golf, but below the other big league sports.

National Collegiate Athletic Association (NCAA)

Collegiate sports also make for very popular betting leagues. In fact, in the same 2022 survey by CRG Global for Variety Intelligence Platform, 34% of online gamblers in the US said they had bet on college football in the previous month. This put the sport in fourth place overall, above soccer, the NHL, boxing/mixed martial arts, and golf. Another 16% of bettors said they had bet on NCAA basketball.

If the option should eventually become available, Texans would also no doubt bet on collegiate football and basketball.

Professional Golfers’ Association (PGA)

While less popular than the Big Four professional team sports, golf creates its own brand of dedicated sports betting aficionados. In addition, Texas hosts several PGA events — including the Valero Texas Open and the AT&T Byron Nelson — which may help spur interest in golf betting if the state decides to legalize it.

Other Sports

The NBA, NFL, MLB, and college football are likely to garner the most interest if sports betting becomes legal in the state. However, Texas sportsbooks will also likely allow wagering on a variety of other leagues.

Interest in soccer betting in Texas may get a boost thanks to the state’s four professional soccer teams: the FC Dallas Toros, the Austin FC Verde, and the Houston Dynamo FC of the MLS, plus the Houston Dash of the NWSL.

In other states, online bettors wager on mixed martial arts (MMA), the Ultimate Fighting Championship (UFC), and boxing. Sportsbooks in Texas are likely to include these options, plus some international and niche sports such as cricket, rugby, and Formula 1.

Legal Sports Betting Options in Texas


Unlike the majority of US states, Texas does not have any legal sports betting options. Online and retail sportsbooks are illegal under state law, as is any kind of casino gambling inside state lines. The one exception is for a handful of tribal casinos, which operate legally under federal law.

The only true casino in the state is run by the Kickapoo Traditional Tribe of Texas, but its location on the Mexican border makes it out of the way for most state residents.

The state is home to three types of gambling: the Texas lottery, horse racing, and standalone poker clubs. Making in-person purchases of lottery draw game tickets and scratchers is legal in Texas. Wagering on horse races is as well.

In-state poker clubs have so far found a way around Texas anti-gambling laws by meeting “social gambling” criteria: games take place in a private location, all players have an equal chance of winning, and no third parties outside the game can profit from it.

As a result of the limited legal betting options within Texas, some residents choose to visit casinos in neighboring states to bet on sports. Louisiana and Arkansas launched legal sportsbooks in 2022, while New Mexico’s tribal casinos have been accepting in-person bets since 2018.

Other Texas residents are choosing to visit tribal casinos in Oklahoma along the border with Texas. In November 2023, the Choctaw Casino & Resort in Durant, OK, began to offer Dallas Cowboys-themed slot machines, presumably to appeal to Texas residents looking to gamble.

A month later, the Chickasaw Nation debuted a mobile app at the Winstar World Casino and Resort in Thackerville, OK. The app allows bettors to play games online while visiting their casino in person. Sports betting is not currently an option, but that may change if Oklahoma legalizes online sportsbooks.

Mobile and Online Sports Betting in Texas


Both in-person and online sports betting are illegal in Texas as of 2023. However, over 1 million Texans attempted to bet at legal sportsbooks in other states between September 1 and October 23, 2023. That marked an increase of 68.5% over the year before. Over 20,000 of those attempts came in a three-day timespan during the American League Championship Series between the Texas Rangers and Houston Astros. These attempts were blocked.

Offshore casinos and sportsbooks based outside of the country often advertise to bettors in the United States. However, these websites operate outside of American jurisdiction, which means they may not have the player protection measures that you would expect of a licensed sportsbook within national borders. If you have a dispute (or if the offshore casino closes without returning your money), you may not have any legal recourse.

Eligibility and Participation in Texas Sports Betting


At this time, no one may legally participate in sports betting in the state of Texas (with the exception of horse racing). In the rest of the country, most legal betting states set the minimum age at 21. However, a handful of jurisdictions allow those 18+ to gamble on sports.

If sports betting becomes legal in Texas, the state may set the minimum age at 18 or 21. Most legalized forms of gambling in the state, including the lottery and horse racing, have a minimum age of 18. However, the only full-service casino in Texas — located on tribal land in Eagle Pass — requires players to be 21.

The most recent proposal to legalize sports betting in the Lone Star State also set the minimum age at 21, although this proposal did not receive a vote in the State Senate.

How To Start Betting in Texas


If you want to start betting in Texas, you have only a few legal options. You can buy a lottery ticket in person, you can bet at a horse race track in person, or you can visit one of the casinos run by tribal entities.

Another exception is for social gambling, such as charity auctions or bingo games, but this doesn’t fall under the umbrella of sports betting, and the house cannot take a percentage of the proceeds. Where private poker clubs fall in terms of legality is currently under debate.

If you want to start betting online in Texas, you have no legal options sanctioned by the state. If Texas online sports betting eventually becomes legal, however, you may wish to know how to place a bet. Now you can browse the available sports, leagues, and games offered by the sportsbook. Understanding Texas Sports Betting Laws


Sports betting is illegal in Texas, whether online or at retail establishments. You cannot legally bet at out-of-state sportsbooks either, which will use geo-validation technology to detect your location and block you if you are outside of state lines. While bettors in Texas may have online access to offshore sportsbooks, these sites may not follow the same player protection protocols as online casinos licensed by US states.

Thanks to the Professional and Amateur Sports Protection Act (PASPA), sports betting was effectively banned nationwide for two decades. In 2018, however, the Supreme Court struck down the law, changing the sports betting environment across the United States. Since then, states have been allowed to set their own gambling rules. At least 38 states have legalized some form of sports betting.

As of November 20, 2023, a dozen states continue to prohibit sports betting in any form. Texas is one of those states.

Betting Restrictions in Texas


Bettors in Texas do not have access to legal sportsbooks in the state. If the state government approves a measure to legalize gambling, Texans will likely have the option to wager on professional, collegiate, and Olympic sports. In the meantime, let’s look at where the state falls on other types of betting.

Texas Lottery

Texas has something in common with other US states by permitting a lottery to operate within state lines. Texas allows both scratch tickets and draw games, as well as Texas-only drawings and multistate drawings. Players must be 18 or older and must purchase tickets in person. No online lottery options are available.

Horse Racing

Since 1987, Texas has permitted betting on horse racing and pari-mutuel wagering. However, only on-site betting by those 18 and over is allowed. In addition to live races, tracks offer simulcast wagering year-round on races around the country. Racetracks and simulcast facilities in Texas include the following:

  • Sam Houston Race Park: Houston
  • Retama Park: Selma (near San Antonio)
  • Gillespie County Fair and Festivals: Fredericksburg
  • Lone Star Park: Grand Prairie

Greyhound Racing

Texas technically permits legal betting on dog races; however, the state does not currently have any active race tracks. A few facilities offer the opportunity to bet on simulcast greyhound races in other states, but greyhound racing has decreased in popularity nationwide amid animal welfare concerns.

Esports

Esports betting allows people to wager on video games, often as competitors play them live. Should Texas approve sportsbooks, it is not yet clear whether they will include esports.

Entertainment

Some sportsbooks in the United States set odds for awards shows and other entertainment events. If Texas legalizes sportsbooks, they may also allow betting on such events.

Politics

It is illegal to bet on politics in the United States. Therefore, even if Texas legalizes sportsbooks, betting on political outcomes, like presidential elections, will not be legal.

Financial Aspects of Texas Sports Betting


In the United States in 2022, sportsbook revenue topped $7.5 billion while the handle (total amount wagered) surpassed $93 billion. In the case of Texas, the second-most populous US state, legal sports betting could generate as much as an estimated $2 billion in revenue and $30 million in wagers during its first year. This would make Texas the highest-grossing sports betting market in the nation.

But what would this mean for taxes on gambling winnings?

State Taxes

Depending on the decided tax rate, Texas could potentially stand to benefit from hundreds of millions of dollars in tax revenue. States vary dramatically in their rate of taxation for gambling activities. On the low end, Nevada and Iowa tax sports betting at only 6.75%. On the high end, New York and New Hampshire tax sports betting at 51% of winnings. Some states charge different tax rates for bets placed online vs. in person.

States also allocate sports betting tax revenues to different causes. Michigan uses sports betting revenue for its School Aid Fund, while also allocating $2 million annually to help firefighters undergoing treatment for cancer. Nevada places the revenue in a state general fund, with special emphasis on education and health services.

Texas tends to avoid excessive taxes. In fact, it is one of nine U.S. states with no state income tax. Recent proposals to legalize sports betting in Texas have set the tax rate at a relatively modest 10% or 15%.

Federal Taxes

When it comes to federal taxes, the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) has set the tax rate for gambling winnings at a flat 24%. This means if Texas residents win a “substantial amount of money” at a legal gambling establishment — such as an in-state lottery or an out-of-state casino — the establishment will automatically deduct 24% of the winnings. The casino, lottery commission, or sportsbook will then send payment to the IRS along with Form W-2G for documentation.

What is a substantial amount of money? It depends on the type of gambling activity. For lotteries, including the Texas Lottery, US citizens and residents must win at least $5,000 in gross proceeds before the tax kicks in. To calculate gross proceeds, you deduct the amount you wagered from the total amount of your winnings. State lotteries and racetracks must report all winnings that surpass either $600 or 300 times your initial stake to the IRS.

To report your gambling income and the taxes you already paid, include it on your Form 1040 tax return under “Other Income.” Remember that the 24% deducted from your winnings was an estimated tax, and the real amount owed will depend on your income tax bracket for the year.

The Future of Sports Betting in Texas


If you’ve asked yourself, “When will sports betting be legal in Texas?” there is no definitive answer yet. The only certainty is that it will not be before 2025. So far, there has been vocal support and opposition on both sides.

Many professional Texas sports teams — including the Dallas Mavericks, the Dallas Cowboys, and the Texas Rangers — have expressed their support for legalizing sports betting in the state. Other teams have already partnered with online sportsbooks, including the following:

  • In 2021, Austin FC of Major League Soccer (MLS) announced a deal with PointsBet.
  • In 2022, Houston Dynamo FC of MLS partnered with Fubo Gaming (Fubo Sportsbook).
  • In 2022, the Houston Astros announced a partnership with sports betting and iGaming operator BetMGM.
  • The Rush Street (BetRivers) sports betting and online casino company became a flagship sponsor of the Frisco Fighters of the Indoor Football League (IFL) at the beginning of the 2022 season.

When it comes to Texas residents, the majority also seems to support legalization. A 2021 poll by The Dallas Morning News and UT Tyler found that 43% favor sports betting in the state and 31% are ambivalent about it. That means that nearly three-quarters (74%) approve or don’t care about legalizing sports betting, while only around a quarter (26%) actively oppose it.

Democrat and Republican poll respondents tended to have the same opinion of sports betting. Of Democrats, 47% supported betting on sporting events, as did 42% of Republicans. For both groups, 27% said they did not care.

When it comes to the state legislature, however, opinions (and votes) tend to follow party lines. Republicans currently hold slight majorities in the Texas Senate and House. In addition, both Lt. Gov. Patrick and Governor Greg Abbot are Republicans. Gov. Abbot has expressed his opposition to legalizing sports betting, and Lt. Gov. Patrick has repeatedly said that enough Texas legislators do not support legalization for it to pass.

If Texas becomes more bipartisan in the coming elections, it may pave the way for legalized sportsbooks in the state. Until that happens, however, legal sports betting in Texas faces an uncertain future.

Responsible Gaming and Player Protection


If you want to place wagers on games or other events, you should follow basic safe gaming practices to protect yourself:

  • Betting is entertainment, not a replacement for other sources of income. Don’t try to earn money for your basic living expenses by gambling. If you do so, you risk getting even deeper into a financial hole.
  • Don’t use money you need for daily expenses to bet on games.
  • Set a limit for the number of bets you place and the amount of money you spend gambling. You can set daily, weekly, or monthly limits.
  • Big payouts often come with big risks. If you decide to bet on a parlay or another wager with long odds, you must prepare yourself to lose the money you spent rather than crossing your fingers for a win.
  • Always do your research: check out the betting lines at multiple sportsbooks, don’t pick the home team just for sentimental reasons, and avoid betting on sports you know nothing about.
  • Keep your funds, your identity, and yourself safe by only placing bets at reputable and licensed establishments. If you have access to an online sportsbook within Texas borders, it is most likely an offshore website that may not have the same safety features or player protection measures in place as US-sanctioned sportsbooks.
  • If gaming is affecting other areas of your life, such as your job or your relationships, seek help from a gaming support organization.

Conclusion and Overview of Texas Sports Betting


If you’ve been asking yourself, “Is online sports betting legal in Texas,” you now know the answer is no. Texas has some of the most restrictive betting laws in the nation. Despite recent attempts to legalize sports betting in the state, sports betting will remain illegal until at least 2025.

Texas residents are free to engage in gambling activities in other states, but in-state options are generally limited to playing the lottery and betting at horse racetracks. Texans who place bets in other jurisdictions (or at state-sanctioned establishments) should know that winnings over a certain amount are subject to federal income tax withholding.

If Texas does eventually legalize sports betting, state residents are likely to prefer wagering on the NFL, NBA, and MLB, along with college football and basketball games. Online sportsbooks are also likely to be popular in Texas, as mobile betting options offer convenience, flexibility, and plentiful opportunities for live betting in the absence of retail establishments.

Until that happens, however, sports fans looking forward to Texas sports betting will simply have to wait for the state laws to change.

What gambling is legal in Texas?

Legal gambling in Texas includes the lottery, horse racing, and dog racing (although the state doesn’t have any operational dog tracks). Some other exceptions exist for social gambling, such as bingo games and private card games. Also, casinos on Native American lands do not fall under the same state jurisdiction as other casinos do.
