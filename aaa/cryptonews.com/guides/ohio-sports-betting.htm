Who Can Partake in Ohio Sports Betting?

Legal Age

Can you sports bet in Ohio if you’re under 21? Unfortunately, no. You must be at least 21 to place a bet with any sportsbook in the state. You can bet on horses, keno, and lotteries if you’re at least 18, but you’ll have to hit the legal drinking age to bet on sports.

Mobile Betting

Mobile sports betting is fully legal in Ohio, so you can conveniently bet on your favorite team right from your tablet or phone.

Remote Registration

There’s no need to head to a retail sportsbook when you sign up for the best sports betting sites. All the sites on our list allow remote registration, and it’s easy to deposit funds and withdraw winnings from any device.

Within State Borders

To place sports bets in Ohio, you must be physically located within state borders. Sportsbooks verify your location by checking the GPS coordinates on your phone.

Retail Sportsbooks

If you’d rather head to a retail sportsbook in Ohio, you have plenty of options. Currently, there are 13 brick-and-mortar sportsbooks scattered throughout the state.

Taxable Winnings

Ohio residents must pay state and federal taxes on sportsbook winnings. You’ll have to pay a 4% state tax, while the federal government taxes you 25% to 28%.

How To Start Betting in Ohio


Sports betting in Ohio is as easy as it is fun. Here’s how to get started.

Select a Sportsbook

Before you get to betting, you’ll have to choose a sportsbook. With dozens of them floating around, this can be tough. Should you pick a new sportsbook with amazing welcome bonuses or a more established betting site with a great reputation?

We suggest picking a sportsbook that offers both. Look for sportsbooks with high welcome bonuses, but keep the platform’s wagering requirements in mind. Wagering requirements outline how many times you must wager your winnings to withdraw the bonus as cash. On some sites, they can be as high as 80x.

Consider user reviews as well. If you see lots of complaints about customer service and payout speed, choose a different sportsbook.

Be sure to pick a sportsbook that accepts your preferred payment method. Some sportsbooks only accept crypto, so if you don’t want to buy currency, these aren’t the right sites for you.

Register Online

Signing up for a sportsbook is nearly as simple as creating a new email account. Just provide your name and contact details, and you’re off to the races. 

Some sportsbooks might require you to prove your identity by sending a copy of your driver’s license. Verification usually happens in 24 to 48 hours.

Telegram sportsbooks don’t require you to make an account at all. You access them through the Telegram app, allowing you to gamble anonymously.

Claim a Bonus

Once you’ve signed up, it’s time to claim your bonus. You may need a promo code to get the bonus (you can usually find one on the sportsbook’s website). Some sportsbooks apply the bonus instantly when you make the required minimum deposit.

Deposit Funds

Next, head to the sportsbook’s banking section to make your first deposit. Here, you’ll see a list of accepted banking methods. These could include crypto, bank transfers, credit/debit cards, and e-wallets.

You’ll have to meet the sportsbook’s minimum deposit amount to use your preferred banking method. For crypto, that’s usually the equivalent of about $3. Minimums range from $10 to $500 for other methods.

Pay attention to deposit fees as well. Most sportsbooks charge a fee of about 6% per transaction for using a credit card.

Choose Your Bet Types

Now, you’ve funded your account and are ready to bet. A few popular betting options include:

  • Parlays: By making a parlay, you can combine two different bets into one.
  • Props: Got a favorite player? With props, you can bet on their performance, such as how many touchdowns or goals they score.
  • Spreads: You can use spreads to bet on how many goals or points a team will get.
  • Totals: This type of bet is one of the easiest to understand. All you have to do is bet on whether the final score will be over or under a certain number.

  • Consider betting for the underdog. They’re less likely to win, but if they do, you can get a big payout.
  • Avoid parlays and longshot bets if you’re just starting out. Both can be a drain on your bankroll if you’re unsure what you’re doing.
  • Take advantage of hot streaks. Sometimes, a player or team consistently goes on a winning streak and can seem to do no wrong. Bet on them, and you have a good chance of a payout.
  • Manage your bankroll carefully. Don’t blow your entire bankroll on a few big bets, especially if you’re new to sports betting.

Reading the Odds

Most sportsbooks offer three types of odds: European, American, and British.

As an Ohio resident, you’re probably most familiar with American odds. Also called money line, these odds represent how much you’d have to wager to win $100 or the amount you’d win for every $100 staked.

British odds are also called fractional odds. They represent the ratio of profit to the amount wagered.

European odds, or decimal odds, represent how much you’d win for every $1 wagered.

What You Can’t Bet on in Ohio

Politics

Although European and U.K. residents can place bets on Ohio political races, residents of Ohio can’t do the same. It’s illegal to bet on political races in Ohio. That means if you want to bet on the next presidential race, you’re out of luck.

Entertainment Award Ceremonies

Gamblers cannot legally bet on entertainment award ceremonies, such as the Oscars or Academy Awards.

Random Determinations

According to the Ohio Casino Control Commission, it’s illegal to bet on random determinations, such as coin flips.

Non-Athlete Performance Criteria

The Commission also disallows betting on non-athlete performance criteria, such as which color shirt a player will wear.

Amateur Athletes

It is illegal to bet on the performance of athletes under 18 in Ohio.

Responsible Gaming in Ohio


Ohio has four regulatory agencies aimed at promoting responsible gambling: the Ohio Casino Control Commission, the Ohio Lottery Commission, the Ohio State Racing Commission, and the Ohio Department of Mental Health and Addiction Services (OhioMHAS).

These agencies offer many responsible gambling tips, including the following:

  • Know when to quit, and don’t chase your losses.
  • Set a betting limit and stick to it.
  • Don’t bet more than you can afford to lose.
  • Don’t gamble when stressed, depressed, or intoxicated.
  • Only play or bet at sites with responsible gambling tools. These tools allow you to place limits on your daily and weekly bets. You can also request a temporary lock on your account to prevent you from gambling.

Ohio Sports Betting History


Curious about how sports betting became legal in Ohio? Check out this condensed timeline:

  • 2018: On May 14, the U.S. Supreme Court struck down the Professional and Amateur Sports Protection Act, which resulted in the lifting of the ban on retail and online sports betting.
  • 2019: Senator John Eklund sponsored SB 111, a bill that would allow casinos to accept wagers on college and professional sports.
  • 2020: A bill to legalize sports wagering passed in the Ohio House of Representatives with a vote of 83-10. However, the bill (HB 194) stalled in the Senate.
  • 2021: Governor Mike DeWine signed HB 121 into law on December 22, 2021.
  • 2022: Retail and online sportsbooks reached deals with Ohio’s casinos and sports franchises and began planning for the January 1, 2023, launch date.
  • 2023: Sixteen sports betting sites went live in Ohio on January 1, with more following in their footsteps in the months to come.
