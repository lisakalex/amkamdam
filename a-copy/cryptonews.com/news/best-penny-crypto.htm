Penny Cryptocurrencies Explained


A penny cryptocurrency is widely defined as a cryptocurrency that is priced in pennies and worth less than $1. The term originates from the stock market, where the equivalents are called penny stocks and were originally defined as anything that traded for “pennies on the dollar.” Penny stocks are defined by the Oxford Dictionary as:

“A common stock being traded at a very low price, investment in which is highly speculative.”

The term penny stocks first emerged around 1921 and was thought to be that what is said above, stocks that cost less than $1. When the SEC was created in 1934, in the aftermath of the stock market crash in 1929, they determined that anything trading for under $5 a share could not be listed on the stock exchange, and it became that anything not traded on the stock exchange was called a penny stock. So that meant that anything trading under $5 was being called a penny stock.

Nowadays, penny stocks and penny cryptocurrencies are defined as assets that are valued in pennies and worth less than a dollar. However, the moniker “penny crypto”, as “penny stock” does, has connotations that go further than just commenting on the price of the asset.

The term penny crypto implies two major things:

  • The potential for huge upsides should that asset gain widespread traction and adoption.
  • The fact that buying the asset is highly speculative and that it is, more than likely, going to fail.

So, while penny cryptocurrencies have huge upside potential, it is highly likely that they will fail. This is why each individual investor should always do their own due diligence and conduct thorough research on the penny cryptos they wish to invest in.

Because of this some would not class established tokens that are worth less than a dollar, mainly Dogecoin and other established meme coin, as penny stocks; saving the term penny stock for coins outside of CoinMarketCap’s top 100 coin. However, this varies by person and many see all cryptos worth less than a dollar, especially meme coins because of their typically speculative nature, as speculative assets.

How to Choose Penny Cryptos To Invest In


As stated above, investors who want to find their “diamond in the rough” and the next big penny crypto should be prepared to conduct a lot of due diligence on their potential investments.

Here we’ll look at the major factors to consider when reviewing penny cryptos. This is by no means an exhaustive list of the items investors should check when researching a project, and each project will have its own unique aspects that should be considered in the context of the project and that market.

Always Beware of Scammers

The cryptocurrency world is still very much unregulated, and its decentralized nature means absolutely anyone can create and release a token and legitimate looking website in under an hour (creating the website is the long part, a token can be created in minutes).

With its open source nature, there is also a wide availability of smart contracts that projects can easily deploy to enhance the look, feel, and legitimacy of their offering. This means that investors need to stay sharp when conducting their research.

Running a penny stock scam was how Jordan Belfort, the protagonist in the biographical film Wolf of Wall Street, made his first fortune.

Project Feasibility and Market

Is the solution the project is proposing a feasible solution that is also needed? Furthermore, is there a market for the proposed solution, who are the competitors, and where are they thinking of launching? (if its a real world project).

Those behind a project should have conducted their research to determine the feasibility of their venture and the potential market for it. They should then present this information to potential investors—i.e., potential token buyers—so they can use it to inform their decisions.

The Team

Who is behind the project? Do they have experience in the field (not always necessary) and are there people on the team with the expertise to build the proposed solution and deploy it to the blockchain? Also, can you verify that the people listed as a part of the team are both real and a part of the project?

Anonymous teams, or anonymous members of a team, are a common occurrence in crypto, and the nature of the industry permits people to remain anonymous. However, investors should be extra cautious of projects with anonymous teams and many will want to see an anonymous team prove their worth before they will invest, typically by showing some form of product.

Token Value as Utility

Here we’re not considering the present or future monetary value of a token, but, instead, does the token have value derived from its utility within the ecosystem or dApp that the project is proposing?

Tokens are a good way of raising funds, but they need to have a future utility that can drive demand for them. Without this, it probably doesn’t have the chance to go 100x like investors are looking for.

There is of course an exception to this—maybe all this—and that is meme coins. Which seem to follow their own unpredictable and still unknown utility-less value trajectories.

Roadmap

Does the roadmap align with the statements made in the whitepaper, and are the development and business items proposed reasonably achievable in the time frame put forth? Many cryptocurrency projects are overly ambitious with the time frames on their roadmaps, in an effort to attract zealous investors looking for quick returns.

Often, while initially less exciting, projects with longer roadmaps are likely better thought out and steered by a team who know what it takes to build such an undertaking and, therefore, can offer a better investment opportunity.

Tokenomics

Tokenomics are a huge part of any project, and investors can sometimes breeze over this section too quickly. How is the token divided between different parts of the business, and why? Are there vesting schedules for tokens designated to teams or early round investors? Is the time and distribution of these tokens open and transparent?

How a token’s supply is utilized by the project can impact the project feasibility, along with the token price. How and when tokens are vested to investors and team members is also an important metric to consider, as the release of a large amount of tokens at once is likely to affect its market price.
