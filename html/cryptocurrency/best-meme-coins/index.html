What Is a Meme Coin?

Meme coins are digital tokens that become popular due to their association with online trends. Initially, meme coins were produced as lighthearted jokes, heavily influenced by internet ideas and culture.

Meme coins generally don’t have any specific utility. Instead, the price of these meme coins is often influenced by social media, discussions on online forums, and even celebrity endorsements.

As we noted above, Dogecoin was the first digital token to call itself a ‘meme coin.’ Following its success, an explosion of meme coins began to appear in the crypto market. Some of these, such as Shiba Inu and Bonk, are well-known among crypto investors and can be easily accessed on the best P2P crypto exchanges.

On the other hand, many meme coins have resulted in investors losing their money. For this reason, investing in meme coins comes with outsized risks as there is no way to predict how the market will move — even in the short term.

8 Ways to Identify New Meme Coins

After trying out dozens of methods for identifying new meme coins, our crypto experts have narrowed them down to the eight most effective ways:

  1. It’s community-driven. Meme coins often gain popularity through social media platforms, forums, and online communities.
  2. There’s a lot of social media hype around it. If you see a cryptocurrency being heavily promoted through memes and online discussions without clear fundamentals, it’s probably a meme coin.
  3. It doesn’t have a real purpose. Most meme coins don’t have a well-defined purpose or technology behind them and seem to be focused more on marketing or entertaining users.
  4. It has funny or quirky branding. Memes, Elon Musk, Japanese dogs — if it’s been in the public eye, someone’s come up with a meme coin for it.
  5. You don’t know who is behind it. Meme coins may lack transparency about their development team or have an anonymous team, rather than one that has been doxxed. An undoxxed team can be a red flag for investors.
  6. It’s very volatile — and you can’t tell what’s causing it. Meme coin prices are driven more by speculation and hype than by their actual value or utility, so they’re usually volatile without a clear reason why.
  7. Its tokenomics are as weird as its branding. Unusual tokenomics, such as a large total supply, a high inflation rate, or other features that deviate from more traditional cryptos are common features of meme coins.
  8. It has a short-term focus. Since they’re often based on a short-term phenomenon, such as a meme, their emphasis is often on quick gains instead of long-term sustainability.
It’s important to note that not all coins with off-beat branding are necessarily meme coins, and some meme coins may evolve over time.

Should I Invest in Meme Coins?

As is evident from our list of upcoming meme coins, only a handful of these tokens come with unique value propositions and many have sudden rises then fall and never return.

This makes it even more challenging to figure out the best meme coins to buy. In the following sections, we’ll discuss why investors might want to consider meme coins.

Meme Coins Could Produce High Profits

Despite their lack of utility, in most cases, meme coins have often been responsible for some of the biggest moves seen in the crypto market. These extreme price shifts often happen due to the interest generated by supporting online communities.

For example, in 2021, Dogecoin saw its price go from around $0.009 to $0.68 — translating to gains of over 7,000%. After reaching its all-time high in 2021, the price of Dogecoin dropped to around $0.17 by the end of 2021. This slump continued well into 2022 and 2023, with the price dropping under $0.06.

However, in the first quarter of 2024, the price started rising again as the new crypto bull run began in earnest, and $DOGE reached $0.2 in March 2024, rewarding true DOGE diamond hands.

More recent examples include Smog, which went from $0.02 to $0.38 in a matter of two weeks — an increase of 1,800%, resulting in huge gains for the earliest investors.

An even more extreme example is dogwifhat, which went from lows of $0.06 to highs of $3.53, sustained by the passionate community behind it. That’s an increase of more than 5,783% — no wonder that since then, other meme coins have been trying to capture some of their own ‘wif’ magic, with investors trying to figure out the crypto with the most potential.

The dark side of this magic formula is that the price can also drop to extreme levels as soon as the frenzy and demand wanes. This is why investors have to be extremely careful when risking their money on meme coins — it’s money they may never see again.

Meme Coins Can Offer Utility

Meme coins, like other cryptocurrencies, can offer utility beyond their initial novelty or entertainment value. While some meme coins may have started as pure silliness, developers and communities sometimes enhance their utility over time.

Some merchants and online platforms may accept meme coins as a form of payment, providing users with a practical way to transact, while other meme coins can incorporate decentralized finance functionalities such as lending, borrowing, yield farming, or staking, providing users with additional financial opportunities.

When looking for the best meme coins for 2024, investors should consider whether the coins have the potential to offer any real utility. Having said that, potential is simply that — potential — and even a meme coin with a solid use case is not a cast-iron promise of its success.

Meme Coins Have Strong Community Backing

When searching for the best meme coins to invest in, investors should attempt to find those with a large community support. As we have pointed out, the community can play a significant role in influencing the price action of a meme coin.

Individuals in the community can help spread the news of the coin, encourage others to invest, or make a token go viral. As such, social media platforms and online forums such as Reddit are good places to spot meme coins that might explode.

However, there’s no guarantee that a meme coin will increase in value exclusively due to its community backing. While there are one or two exceptions, it is a highly risky move to invest in a meme coin just because the project has a lot of followers on social media.

Risks of Investing in Meme Coins

Investing in meme coins can potentially lead to huge gains, but it also comes with risks.

  • Lack of utility: Most meme coins lack any real utilities, with only a few exceptions. The value of a new meme coin is often based entirely on the frenzy fueled by social media platforms, which can fizzle out as quickly as it appears.
  • Extremely volatile: All crypto is volatile to some degree, but meme coins, which rely almost entirely on social media hype and the community’s enthusiasm for it, are even more volatile and unpredictable.
  • Potential scams: Many meme coin projects have turned out to be rug pulls. In other words, ventures where crypto developers abandon a project and run away with investors’ funds.
  • Limited information on the development team: Meme coins may lack transparency about their development team or have an anonymous team, in contrast to reputable cryptos that are usually backed by identifiable development teams.
  • Lack of regulation: Many meme coins operate in a less regulated environment compared to more established cryptos, making them a perfect breeding ground for malicious actors to manipulate markets and engage in fraudulent activities.
  • Poor security measures: Many meme coins are much more concerned with marketing than security, and end up launching without having robust security measures in place. This opens them up to hacking and other security breaches, which puts investors’ funds at risk.

Investors should be extremely careful when looking for the best meme coins to buy for their portfolio. If they manage to buy and sell at the right time, they might be able to make money from crypto meme coins.

But they could also lose all the funds they invested. The best strategy would be to focus on cryptocurrencies that have strong fundamentals that are likely to stand the test of time.

What Influences the Price of a Meme Coin?

As with any other investment, the price of cryptocurrencies is influenced by supply and demand. However, in the case of meme coins, the factors that affect the general market sentiment are slightly different. Here, we’ll discuss the top five factors that influence the price of a meme coin.

Going Viral on Social Media

Going viral on social media is a major driver behind the price spikes of meme coins, often fueled by hype and FOMO. When a meme coin gains traction online, its visibility and trading volume increase, often leading to a rapid rise in price. Influencers and viral posts can amplify interest and investment almost overnight.

To leverage this, monitor platforms like X and Reddit for trending topics. Use social media analytics tools, such as Hootsuite and Brand24, to track mentions and sentiment.

Investing early in meme coins that are gaining online momentum can be profitable. However, always assess the risk, as the hype can fade quickly, potentially leading to sharp price declines.

Overall Market Performance

Overall market performance significantly impacts the price of meme coins. When the broader cryptocurrency market is bullish, meme coins often see amplified gains due to increased investor confidence and speculative trading. Conversely, in bear markets, these coins may experience steeper declines.

To assess and capitalize on market trends, track indices like the total cryptocurrency market cap and Bitcoin’s performance, as these often dictate market sentiment. Utilize crypto tools like TradingView for real-time data and trend analysis.

Buying during uptrends and considering timely exits can maximize returns from meme coins, but careful risk management is essential to navigate their high volatility.

Exchange Listings

When a meme coin is listed on the best cryptocurrency exchanges, it gains increased visibility, accessibility, and credibility, often resulting in a surge in price due to higher trading volumes.

To take advantage of this, stay updated on exchange announcements and potential listings. Consider subscribing to newsletters from major platforms and joining their online communities.

By being among the first to know about upcoming crypto listings, you can buy into relevant meme coins early, potentially capturing gains from initial price spikes.

Token Burning Events

Token burning events are crucial for the price increase of meme coins because they reduce supply, making the currency scarcer. This scarcity can lead to higher prices if demand remains steady or increases. Such events are typical of deflationary cryptocurrencies that employ ways of burning tokens to enhance value.

Investors can track these events by following official coin announcements on social media or dedicated cryptocurrency news sites. To capitalize on token burns, consider buying before announced burns and selling post-event to profit from potential price surges.

Always verify the scale of the burn and its historical impact on the coin’s price to make informed decisions.

Celebrity Endorsements

Celebrity endorsements significantly boost meme coin prices by attracting widespread attention and investment. When well-known crypto influencers and KOLs (i.e., Key Opinion Leaders) publicly support a meme coin, their large followings often lead to increased buying activity, driving up prices.

To leverage this, investors should monitor social media platforms where celebrities and crypto influencers are active. Pay attention to sudden surges in followers or mentions related to specific coins.

Acting quickly on such endorsements can be profitable, but it’s vital to assess the authenticity and potential impact of the endorsement to avoid short-lived hype.
