What Are Crypto Launches?

A crypto launch refers to the introduction of a new crypto project to the market. Newly launched cryptocurrency can be released in any of the following ways:

  1. Initial Coin Offerings (ICOs), Token Sales, or Token Generation Events (TGEs) – The issuing and selling of a new cryptocurrency token to investors and supporters, generally at a discounted rate, to raise capital for the development of a project.

  2. Initial Exchange Offerings (IEOs) – Token sales conducted on cryptocurrency exchanges. The exchange hosts and manages the token sale on behalf of the project.

  3. Security Token Offerings (STOs) – STOs involve the issuance of security tokens, which represent ownership or a stake in an underlying asset. Such tokens are subject to securities regulations.

  4. Airdrops – Crypto airdrops involve the distribution of free tokens to existing cryptocurrency holders. These can be a marketing strategy to create awareness, reward loyal users, or distribute tokens in a decentralized manner.

  5. Forks – When a new version of a cryptocurrency’s protocol is implemented, a fork occurs. This results in a split, creating two separate blockchains with distinct rules.

  6. Project Launches – Beyond token launches, new crypto projects launching soon can introduce new blockchain technologies, platforms, or decentralized applications (dApps) to the market.

What Makes a Coin Pump After Launching

Understanding what drives a coin’s price surge post-launch is crucial for investors. Recognizing these triggers can help in making informed decisions, anticipating market movements, and identifying potential investment opportunities. This section explores key factors that contribute to a coin pump after it enters the market.

Hype and FOMO

Hype and FOMO (i.e., Fear of Missing Out) can significantly drive up a coin’s value post-launch. Emotional trading, spurred by strong marketing and the perception of scarcity, compels investors to buy early to avoid missing potential gains. These factors create a rush of purchases, often resulting in a sharp increase in the coin’s price as demand exceeds supply.

Sold-Out Presales

Cryptos that sell out in presales often have limited initial supply, creating a supply-demand imbalance when they launch on exchanges. This scarcity drives up demand as investors who missed the presale rush to purchase on the open market, often leading to a rapid price increase. A sold-out presale signals strong investor interest and anticipation, adding to the buying frenzy at launch.

Exchange Listing Announcements

Exchange listing announcements can significantly boost a coin’s price as they expand market access and enhance credibility. When a new coin is listed on a major exchange, it attracts a broader audience of traders and investors, leading to increased buying activity. This visibility often results in a sharp price surge due to the perceived increase in legitimacy and potential for wider adoption, making the coin more desirable to purchase.

Trading Volume Competitions

After a coin launches, exchanges may host trading volume competitions, offering rewards like airdropped tokens to active traders. These incentives encourage increased buying and selling, boosting the coin’s trading volume. This heightened activity raises the coin’s market visibility and its perceived value, often leading to a price pump as more traders participate to win rewards, further fueling the demand.

Low Circulating Supply

A low circulating supply can sharply drive up a coin’s price after launching due to scarcity. With fewer tokens available, even moderate demand can lead to significant price increases as buyers compete for a limited supply. This scarcity effect is akin to rare collectibles; the rarer the item, the higher the potential value, attracting more investors seeking potential price appreciations from low supply cryptos.

How to Decide Whether to Invest in a Crypto Launch

Navigating a crypto launch requires strategic decision-making to ensure the viability of your investment. Understanding the fundamentals behind the project and assessing its potential can guide investors towards making more informed choices.

Evaluate the Coin’s Utility

To evaluate a coin’s utility, consider if the token has real-world applications, solves specific problems, or offers unique services. Utility tokens provide more than speculative value; they facilitate transactions or interactions within their ecosystems.

For example, Binance Coin (BNB) reduces trading fees on its platform. This intrinsic utility often underpins long-term value.

Look for Strong Marketing

Investigate the project’s marketing efforts to gauge its visibility and investor interest. Strong marketing can indicate the team’s commitment to growth and outreach.

Check for active social media presence, engagement strategies, and partnerships with reputable companies. Effective marketing can drive demand, boosting the coin’s initial uptake and sustained interest.

Research the Coin Launch Price

Analyzing the launch price involves comparing it with similar projects and assessing the initial market cap. A reasonably set launch price, suggests a grounded approach by the founders. It should align with the project’s scope and market potential, avoiding unrealistic evaluations that deter savvy investors.

Secured Exchange Listings

Find out which exchanges will list the new coin by checking announcements on official project pages or crypto news websites, such as CryptoNews.

Secured listings on prominent exchanges enhance a coin’s credibility and liquidity, making it more accessible to a broader audience. These listings are critical for providing the necessary trading volume that supports price stability.

Team and Developer Transparency

Assessing team transparency involves reviewing the project’s website and communications for detailed information about the founders and developers.

A transparent team discloses their background and track record, which builds trust. Knowing who is behind the project helps investors feel more secure, particularly against potential scams or mismanagement.
