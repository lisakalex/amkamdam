Methodology: How We Ranked Upcoming Binance Listings


To rank new and upcoming Binance listings, we rated them based on five criteria — demand, innovation and use cases, compliance, development team, and fit with Binance’s mission.

Demonstrated Demand and Trading Volume (30%)

Coins that have demonstrated high demand during presale and/or high trading volumes and liquidity on other exchanges have proven market demand. Binance is more likely to list coins that will attract traders to its platform, ensuring robust trading activity.

We examined presale performance, as well as trading volumes and liquidity metrics from other major exchanges, assessing market demand and ensuring that the coins have maintained consistent demand.

Innovative Technology and Use Cases (30%)

Binance favors coins that bring new technology or solutions to the table, particularly those that solve existing problems in novel ways or significantly enhance the blockchain ecosystem’s capabilities.

We scrutinized the underlying technology and the practical applications of each coin, prioritizing those that introduce innovative solutions or substantially improve on existing blockchain functionalities.

Regulatory Compliance (20%)

Coins that are compliant with the stringent regulatory requirements across various jurisdictions stand a better chance of being listed. Binance rigorously ensures that listing a coin will not expose them to legal penalties or restrictions in key markets.

We consulted legal analyses and regulatory updates to verify each coin’s compliance with laws and regulations across multiple jurisdictions, ensuring they meet all necessary legal standards for listing.

Team Credibility and Transparency (20%)

The background, track record, and transparency of the coin’s development team are critically assessed. Binance tends to list projects spearheaded by teams known for their expertise and integrity in the cryptocurrency space.

We assessed the credibility of each project’s team through their historical accomplishments, industry reputation, and the transparency of their operations and communication with the community.

Binance’s Listing Criteria for New Coins


Binance doesn’t just accept any new coin on the market, and tokens that want to list on Binance have to go through rigorous due diligence. While Binance itself states that there are no hard and fast rules, the following elements are the bare minimum that should be in place to fit Binance’s listing criteria for new crypto coins.

  • A proven team, a useful product, and a sizeable user base.
  • A minimum viable product – the application will not pass the initial screening otherwise.
  • Statistics on user adoption – this will help Binance in the screening stage.

You can also take the following steps to enhance your project’s listing prospects:

  • Regularly update the community and Binance on project progress, even after listing.
  • Integrate BNB into your ecosystem or raise BNB during fundraising.
  • Actively support Binance within your community.

Some actions and behaviors can jeopardize the status of a potential listing – here are a few things you shouldn’t do if you’re preparing your coin for listing on Binance.

  • Do not contact Binance’s CEO for application status updates.
  • Do not request a contact person; communication is handled via email to prevent biased influence or potential bribery.
  • Do not promote coins to Binance’s CEO on social media; instead, provide status updates through the official Binance listing application form.
  • Do not send your white paper directly to Binance’s CEO.

How Do Coins Get Listed on Binance?


The project founder or CEO should submit the online application forms for direct listing on Binance.com or Launchpool application. Direct listing and Launchpool are open to projects with circulating tokens or those without an issued coin.

All relevant information should be included in the form. The due diligence process is extremely thorough, so being patient, responsive, and professional will help speed things up.

If the project passes the initial review, Binance’s team will contact the team behind the project. It’s important to note that contact from Binance does not guarantee listing. Strict NDAs are mandatory, and project teams cannot disclose listing information until Binance issues an official announcement.

Project owners who spread fear, uncertainty, and doubt (FUD) or negative comments about Binance could see their entire project blacklisted. Aggressive coin shilling can also hurt the listing process. You might not be blacklisted for this, but it can still slow down or put the whole process in danger.

Where to Find Binance New Listings


Once Binance new listings are approved, they’re displayed on the Binance Launchpad or Binance Innovation Zone, depending on the stage of development and risk profile of the new cryptocurrencies. Let’s take a closer look at each option.

Binance Launchpad

Created in 2018, Binance Launchpad is a Binance-owned crowdfunding platform for new cryptocurrencies. Tokens that are added to the launchpad can raise funds from investors at a fixed token price, similar to a crypto presale. Once the project’s fundraising goal has been reached, it typically lists on the main Binance exchange.

Cryptocurrency projects can apply to join Binance Launchpad early in their lifecycle. Launchpad selects projects based on a project’s technology, its potential market demand, and the quality of the project team.

Past tokens that have listed on Binance through the Binance Launchpad include $XRP, $MATIC, and $CAKE.

Binance Innovation Zone

The Binance Innovation Zone is a section of Binance dedicated to new cryptocurrencies. It typically features high-risk tokens with small market caps. Many successful presale cryptos that list on Binance initially do so by listing in the Innovation Zone.

All Binance users have access to trade tokens in the Innovation Zone. However, by giving these coins their own space in the exchange, Binance intends to highlight the fact that they are relatively new and may have higher volatility than more established tokens.

How Often Does Binance Add New Coins?


Binance is often viewed as the go-to exchange to invest in brand-new coins that are still in their infancy. However, this isn’t the case at all — at least in today’s market. For example, in the six months prior to writing this guide, Binance added just six new coins to its exchange.

This illustrates that Binance is not as active in the listing department as it once was, meaning that it now only considers high-quality projects.

Nonetheless, Binance appears to be more focused on adding additional markets to its exchange, relevant to coins that are already listed. For example, in recent months, Binance has added a significant number of futures and other derivative markets, in addition to support for more emerging fiat currencies such as TRY and ZAR.
