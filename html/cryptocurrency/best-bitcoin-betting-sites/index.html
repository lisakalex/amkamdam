Is Crypto Betting Legal?

Many jurisdictions around the world have strict laws surrounding gambling, especially when it comes to online operators. In the USA, for example, sports betting only became legal in 2018 but was worth as much as $125 billion in 2022 — a figure that is predicted to grow even further by the end of the decade. Gambling is legal in the UK, but there are strict rules and regulations that bookmakers must follow to get a license, particularly when it comes to advertising.

However, cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin and Ethereum seem to sit outside of conventional gambling laws, despite crypto regulation becoming a hot-button topic for many governments. The overarching reason for this is how cryptocurrencies are defined. As very few governments define Bitcoin and other digital assets as legal tender, they typically do not fall within the remit of online gambling controls.

This subsequently means that crypto gambling sites often accept players from most countries. This is the case as long as the cryptocurrency casino does not have a relationship with fiat money. However, players are advised to assess the legality of gambling online with cryptocurrencies before proceeding.

Possible Downsides of Betting With Crypto

While betting with cryptocurrency offers numerous advantages, you have to consider possible drawbacks too. Here are some potential threats to keep in mind:

Crypto betting is still somewhat unregulated

The decentralized nature of cryptocurrencies means that they operate outside traditional regulatory bodies, leaving users vulnerable to potential risks such as fraud, money laundering, and market manipulation. Bettors may find it challenging to get legal assistance or protection if they have a dispute with the crypto betting site.

Crypto transactions are irreversible

Unlike traditional banking transactions, cryptocurrency transactions are irreversible once confirmed on the blockchain. Accidental transfers or erroneous wagers cannot be reversed, potentially leading to financial losses if proper care is not taken during the betting process.

Cryptocurrency is volatile

The value of popular cryptocurrencies can experience extreme fluctuations within a matter of days or even hours. Your crypto winnings may fluctuate in value significantly before they are withdrawn or exchanged for fiat currency, and assessing the true value of betting returns and managing financial risks can be more challenging.
