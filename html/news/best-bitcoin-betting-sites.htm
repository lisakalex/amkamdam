Is Crypto Betting Legal?

Many jurisdictions around the world have strict laws surrounding gambling – especially when it comes to online operators. In the USA, for example, sports betting only became legal in 2018 but was worth as much as $125 billion in 2022 – a figure that is predicted to grow even further by the end of the decade.

Gambling is 100% legal in the UK, but there are strict rules and regulations that bookmakers must follow to get a license, particularly when it comes to advertising. However, cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin and Ethereum seem to sit outside of conventional gambling laws, despite crypto regulation becoming a hot-button topic for many governments.

The overarching reason for this is how cryptocurrencies are defined. As very few governments define Bitcoin and other digital assets as legal tender, they typically do not fall within the remit of online gambling controls. This subsequently means that Bitcoin gambling sites often accept players from most countries. This is the case as long as the cryptocurrency casino does not have a relationship with fiat money. 

However, players are advised to assess the legality of gambling online with cryptocurrencies before proceeding.
