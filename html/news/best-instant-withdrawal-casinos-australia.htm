How do Online Casino Payouts Work?


Most online casinos in Australia follow a similar process when it comes to payouts. First, players must request a withdrawal from within their account dashboard. The amount must be stated and the funds will need to be transferred to the same payment method.

This ensures the casino complies with anti-money laundering (AML) regulations. After all, ASIC recently fined Star Entertainment Group $150 million for AML violations. Once the player confirms the payout request, it will then be sent to the casino’s payment team. Our research shows that on average, it takes 24-48 hours for online casinos to approve payouts.

Additional delays can occur if the request is made over the weekend. The casino will manually check the withdrawal and the respective player’s account. Once satisfied everything is in check, they will process the withdrawal. If the funds are being sent back to a debit/credit card, it will likely take an additional 48 hours to arrive. Bank payments can take even longer.

In addition, the first withdrawal made by players is often the slowest. This is because you can usually open an account and deposit funds without needing to upload ID verification documents. However, requests for documents will arrive when you make the initial withdrawal request.

The KYC process requires a government-issued ID and a recently-issued proof of address. Once submitted, you’ll need to wait for the casino to verify them. This can add further delays, as it can take a few days for the documents to be approved. And, if there are any issues (such as an unclear document), you’ll need to submit the documents again.

Now consider the payout process when using a cryptocurrency-centric casino, such as Lucky Block or Mega Dice. As soon as the payout request is made, it will be approved instantly. The cryptocurrency tokens will be transferred to the player’s wallet. It’s important the correct wallet address is entered, as mistakes cannot be rectified once the transfer is made.

Bitcoin, for example, is one of the slowest blockchains, so you’ll need to wait around 10 minutes. Other cryptocurrencies, like Dogecoin, Shiba Inu, and Tether are considerably faster.
