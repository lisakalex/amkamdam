Crypto Wallets Explained 

Choosing a suitable crypto wallet is crucial. Investors will need to select a wallet that offers adequate security controls, a user-friendly interface, and convenient accessibility. In addition, investors will need to consider what features they seek from a wallet, such as being able to trade or stake tokens. 

Crypto wallets come with many different functions, but they are primarily used to store digital currencies. For example, consider an investor who buys Bitcoin from a crypto exchange in South Africa. Instead of trusting the exchange to keep the tokens safe, many investors will withdraw the funds to a private wallet. 

In doing so, the investor can retain full control of their cryptocurrencies. In addition, crypto wallets allow investors to send and receive funds. Transactions are conducted on a wallet-to-wallet basis and do not go through a centralized entity, such as a bank or e-wallet provider. Instead, wallet transactions are private and decentralized. 

When sending funds to another person, the user will need the receiver’s wallet address. No two wallet addresses are the same. After confirming the transaction, it will be executed by the underlying network. 

The best crypto wallets in South Africa come with additional features, allowing users to fully engage with the blockchain ecosystem. 

  • For example, wallet providers like Best Wallet and Binance support trading. This means that users can buy, sell, and trade cryptocurrencies without leaving their wallet. 
  • Providers like Trust Wallet and OKX come with staking tools, enabling users to earn passive income on their idle tokens. 

As we explain in more detail later, there are many different types of crypto wallets. For instance, non-custodial wallets provide users with their private keys. This means the user has full control of their crypto tokens. 

Custodial wallets, on the other hand, remove the need to secure private keys. This is because the custodian will keep the crypto tokens safe on behalf of investors. Investors also need to consider their preferred device type, such as a mobile or desktop wallet. 

Do You Need a Crypto Wallet? Key Reasons Explained 

There are various reasons why a crypto wallet is crucial when investing in digital currencies. This is the case for both short and long-term traders. 

Discussed below are the key benefits provided by the best crypto wallets in South Africa. 

Safely Store Cryptocurrencies

Cryptocurrencies are highly sought after by cybercriminals, due to their anonymous characteristics. Put otherwise, if an investor has their crypto tokens stolen remotely, the likelihood is that the funds will never be recovered. This is why wallets are so important, as they keep cryptocurrencies safe. 

The level of security offered by a wallet will vary greatly. 

For example, ‘cold’ wallets are always offline, so offer the greatest security controls. ‘Hot’ wallets are less secure as they are always connected to live servers. But hot wallets can still be protected by two-factor authentication and other security tools. 

Nonetheless, crypto wallets allow South Africans to store wealth away from banks and other financial institutions. 

Send and Receive Cryptocurrencies

Wallets also allow users to transact, often without a centralized intermediary. Transactions are processed on a wallet-to-wallet basis via public addresses. 

  • For example, suppose an investor wants to transfer some Bitcoin tokens to a family member. 
  • After providing the receiver’s wallet address, the Bitcoin transaction should be confirmed by the blockchain in about 10 minutes. 

If the investor wishes to receive Bitcoin from another person, they would need to provide their unique wallet address. 

Additional Features

The best crypto wallets for South African traders come with additional functionality. This removes the need to use third parties when engaging with the blockchain ecosystem. 

  • Some wallets allow users to swap tokens instantly. For example, swapping Bitcoin for Dogecoin without needing to use an exchange. 
  • Earning passive income is also possible when using a top-rated wallet. The most common methods include staking and yield farming. 
  • Many wallets are able to connect to Web3 applications. This includes decentralized exchange and lending platforms, offering a world of possibilities for South African investors. 

While security should be the main priority when choosing a wallet, investors should also explore what features are available.

How do Crypto Wallets Work? 

Looking for some guidance on how crypto wallets actually work?

Here’s what South African investors need to know before getting started. 

Wallet Addresses

Once a suitable wallet has been chosen, the investor will need to set it up. This will generate a new, unique wallet address. The wallet address allows the investor to receive cryptocurrencies from another location. 

Think of it like an electronic funds transfer (EFT), which requires the sender to specify the receiver’s personal account number. The wallet address is safe to share with other people, as it simply allows the user to receive tokens via the blockchain. 

Each blockchain network has its own wallet address standard. For example, Ethereum wallets always start with 0x. Nevertheless, if the wrong wallet address is provided, the tokens will be sent to an incorrect location. This means the tokens will be unrecoverable. 

Private Keys

If the investor opts for a non-custodial wallet, they will be provided with unique private keys when getting started. This is usually provided as a 12-word passphrase, but some wallets use a much longer combination. 

Either way, private keys must never be shared with another person. Nor should they be kept on a device that connects to the internet. 

If they end up in the wrong hands, the private keys will allow an unauthorized person to access the wallet. This is one of the most common reasons for crypto wallets being hacked remotely. 

Custodial wallets do not provide users with their private keys. This is because the custodian is responsible for keeping the cryptocurrencies safe. Custodial wallets are popular with beginners, but investors should ensure the provider is regulated. 

Wallet Fees

Software wallets are usually free to use. There are no fees to store or receive cryptocurrencies. But fees are charged when sending cryptocurrencies to another wallet. 

The best crypto wallets in South Africa do not charge a markup on network fees. Instead, fees go directly to blockchain miners. 

Fees are quoted in real-time and rise and fall based on network demand. 

Crypto hardware wallets, however, are not free. Prices vary depending on the provider and model type. For example, Trezor charges $69 and $219 for its basic and advanced models.

Hardware Wallets

A more secure option is to buy a Bitcoin hardware wallet. As noted above, prices will vary, with Trezor charging $69 for its basic device.

Hardware wallets come with the added benefit of cold storage. In simple terms, this means the cryptocurrencies and private keys are never exposed to live internet servers. 

Safeguards are also in place to protect investors from physical theft. This is because cryptocurrencies can only leave the hardware wallet if the PIN is entered on the device itself.  

Paper Wallets

We also found that paper wallets are increasingly becoming popular with South African investors. After generating a public address and private key, the user will print the credentials. 

This keeps the wallet offline at all times. It is imperative that the paper wallet is kept somewhere safe. It is also important to ensure the paper wallet provider is legitimate and that private keys are encrypted when initially shown to the user. 

Both paper and hardware wallets offer excellent security, but do make it cumbersome to send and trade cryptocurrencies.
