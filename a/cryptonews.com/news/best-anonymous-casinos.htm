How do Crypto Casinos Allow You to Play Anonymously?

The traditional gambling space operates in a strict regulatory environment. At the forefront of this are anti-money laundering (AML) and know-your-customer (KYC) regulations. This requires regular online casinos to verify all registered players. Not only in terms of personal information and contact details but KYC documents, such as a passport and proof of address.

So that begs the question – how are crypto casinos able to offer anonymous and KYC-free accounts? The simple answer is the way that crypto assets like Bitcoin are defined around the world. With just two exceptions – the Central African Republic and El Salvador, crypto assets are not defined as legal tender.

In turn, this means that traditional gambling regulations do not cover crypto assets. Instead, legislation is specifically related to ‘fiat money’, such as US dollars, British pounds, etc. So by depositing, wagering, and withdrawing cryptocurrencies at an online casino, no KYC requirements are triggered.

Free Spins

Anonymous casinos are also known for giving players free spins. These are used on slot games and are risk-free.

For example, Lucky Block offers 50 free spins when the first deposit is at least €20. If the 50 free spins do not yield any winnings, the player hasn’t lost anything.

Just like matched deposits, free spins also need to be wagered. The wagering amount is based on the winnings made from the free spins. For example, suppose the player gets 30 free spins and wins $200. If the wagering requirement is 10x, the player needs to wager $2,000 on the casino before cashing out the winnings.

No Deposit Bonuses

Looking for an anonymous Bitcoin casino with no deposit bonus?

In a nutshell, anonymous casinos will rarely offer no deposit bonuses. After all, what’s stopping a player from opening an unlimited number of accounts to keep claiming the no deposit bonus?

That said, some crypto casinos will offer a no deposit bonus if the player completes a KYC process. This would require documentation, such as a passport and utility bill.

Deposit Reloads

Reload bonuses are aimed at existing players that make regular deposits. They work the same as matched deposit bonuses but often come with less generous terms.

Key Terms and Conditions to Look For

While the bonuses offered by anonymous casinos can be lucrative, it is important to read through the key terms and conditions before proceeding.

The most important metrics to look for are:

  • Wagering Requirement: As mentioned, most casino bonuses come with a wagering requirement. This will need to be met before the bonus funds, and winnings can be withdrawn.
  • Time Limit: Bonuses usually come with a time limit. This is the number of days that the wagering requirement needs to be met before the bonus funds expire. The shorter the time limit, the harder it will be to convert the bonus into withdrawable winnings.
  • Eligible Games: Casinos will usually have a list of eligible and ineligible games that the bonus funds can be used on. There will also be a ‘contribution’ ratio for each game type. In most cases, slots continue 100% to the wagering requirement. While table games and live dealers might only contribute 5%.
  • Wagering Limits: Some anonymous casino bonuses will come with wagering limits. This is the maximum amount that will contribute to the wagering requirement on any given bet. For example, suppose the limit is $100, but the player bets $200. While the $200 will be accepted, only $100 will go towards the wagering target.
  • Maximum Wins: We also found that some bonuses come with a maximum win amount. For example, suppose the maximum is $1,000, but the player lands a $10,000 jackpot. This means that if bonus funds were used, they will only receive $1,000 from the casino.
  • Anonymous Casino Promo Codes: Some casino bonuses might require players to enter a promo code. If so, and the promo code isn’t entered, they will not receive the bonus.

The best anonymous casinos clearly state the above terms and conditions next to the bonus offer. This prevents the need to read through countless pages of complex legal jargon.
